# Messaging v1 quick guides

These one-page guides use school-friendly English. Arabic localization should preserve the product terms **Messages**, **School Chats**, **Safeguarding**, and **System Owner** and should be reviewed by a fluent education/safeguarding practitioner before publication.

## 1. Messaging basics for teachers

Open **Messages**, choose a class or permitted person, open the conversation, and send text, up to five protected photos, or one voice note. A message is **Sent** when CHH commits it, **Delivered** when the receiving participant acknowledges it, and **Read** only when the school policy allows read receipts. Never use messaging for an emergency. Do not share passwords, medical detail beyond the school-approved minimum, or content about another child.

## 2. School Chats for parents

In Family Hero Hub, open a child and choose **School Chats**. The school controls who may contact you and whether replies are open. Photos and voice notes are protected and open inside the app. If a chat is read-only or closed, contact the school through its published route. School Chats are not an emergency channel.

## 3. Notification settings

Allow Android notifications when prompted. Notifications use generic wording and should not contain message bodies. Signing out revokes this installation's token. If notifications stop, open the app, confirm the account and child, then re-enable notifications in Android settings. iOS/APNs is not part of this pilot.

## 4. Contact hours

Messages may still be sent outside contact hours, but non-urgent family-to-school notifications can be held until the next open period. Staff should use **urgent** only under school policy. Contact hours delay notifications; they do not delete or hide committed messages.

## 5. Safeguarding review in five minutes

Open **Safeguarding**, find the conversation using safe metadata, choose a reason category, write a meaningful justification, acknowledge protected access, and start the time-limited review. Review mode is audited and does not make you a participant, create receipts, change unread counts, or send notifications. End the review when finished.

## 6. Restricting or closing a conversation

From an active safeguarding review, select a restriction or closure, record the confidential reason, and optionally add neutral participant wording. Check the affected reply direction before confirming. Closure and reopening are audited. Use the least restrictive action that addresses the concern.

## 7. Internal notes and flags

Use a **flag** to track a concern and its severity/status. Use an **internal note** for append-only safeguarding context; correct an earlier note by adding a correction, not editing it. These records are not shown to ordinary participants or FHH. Do not put speculation or unnecessary personal data into a note.

## 8. Evidence export

Start an authorised review, choose internal evidence export, state the formal purpose, and request the package. Generation runs in a durable worker and is not ready until integrity verification succeeds. Download through CHH only; downloads are audited and limited. Store the package in the approved case system, record its SHA-256 hashes, and never email it as an attachment.

## 9. Safeguarding access management

Grant only the minimum permission needed: review, moderation, export, internal-note export, legal holds, or access management. State a reason, verify the school and staff member, and review access periodically. A school role alone does not grant safeguarding access. Removing a grant prevents new protected actions and downloads.

## 10. System Owner responsibilities

The **System Owner** is the school-nominated operational lead; the title does not imply principalship or legal ownership. They invite staff/admins, manage access and settings, approve retention operations, and explicitly transfer ownership. Keep a second active administrator available for recovery. Never deactivate the current owner before a confirmed transfer.
